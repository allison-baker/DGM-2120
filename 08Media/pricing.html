Pricing

One of the hardest things to figure out on the business end when starting out in photography is pricing your services. There are so many factors to consider, including but not limited to what kind of photography do you want to shoot? How long will your shoots be? How many photos will you return to clients in their galleries? How many outfit changes and location changes are you going to have per shoot and will more cost extra? What are other photographers in your area charging and what are they including in their packages that makes hiring them over you more appealing, and how can you switch that around to work in your favor? What's your audience? Are you willing to offer discounts and sales? How can you make sure you charge correctly for your skill level?

My Pricing

I think it's very good practice as a businessperson to be transparent about your prices. People are more likely to hire you if they can know from the start what they are going to be paying. For this reason, I have the prices for all of the different kinds of shoots I offer displayed on the booking page of my website.

I am still a relatively new photographer and have been in business for less than 5 years. I also am not doing photography as my full time job as I plan to go into web design. I offer many different kinds of shoots and mainly get hired by friends, family, and through recommendation from past clients. For all of these reasons, my prices are likely lower than many other photographers you may have seen and that's alright with me. My prices are higher than what they used to be as I have gained experience, but still lower than the market average. For you to reference as you price your business, I've placed my prices below:

Shoot Type Base Cost Audience
Headshots $65 Actors, performers
Portraits $80 Seniors, LDS Missionaries, creatives
Couples/Bridals $100 Engagement announcements, wedding announcements
Groups $120 Families, college roommates, friend groups
Events $50 hourly Family reunions, conventions, protests/rallies

The prices in the table above are my base costs for different types of shoots. Each package includes one outfit/location and a return of all good photos from the shoot. Some photographers will return a limited number of photos, but I have decided that I like to return all of the photos that turn out well because I want my clients to have as many options as possibe. I also know if it were me as a customer, I would want more photos returned if possible. I charge a little extra for more outfits and locations, and for more extensive editing like in-depth retouching. Events I book on a case by case basis as I am not primarily an event photographer, so I will shoot events that interest me on a volunteer basis or for an hourly rate. I've decided that I am not interested in shooting weddings, so I don't even list them as a pricing option on my site.

As you can see, packages and pricing are highly customizable to each photographer, and you can pick and choose what types of shoots you are interested in offering as well. I know it's difficult to get a price nailed down when it depends on so many factors and there's no right answer, but I hope this page helped you a little bit in your decision making process.
